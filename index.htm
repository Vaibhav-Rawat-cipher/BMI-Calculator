Things you need to know before you know your BMI:

-BMI is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. It is calculated by dividing a person's weight in kilograms by the square of their height in meters.

-BMI is used to categorize individuals into different weight categories, such as underweight, normal weight, overweight, and obese.

-BMI is not a perfect measure of body fat and may not accurately reflect the health of individuals with high muscle mass or those with certain medical conditions.

-BMI is a useful screening tool for assessing weight status and potential health risks, but it should be used in conjunction with other assessments, such as waist circumference and body composition analysis.

-BMI is not a diagnostic tool and should not be used to diagnose or treat any medical conditions.

BMI Categories

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI 18.5 - 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI 25 - 29.9
  • Obesity: BMI 30 or greater
